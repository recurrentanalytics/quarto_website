# Reading Notes Guide

Reading notes are part of the [Notes & Knowledge Base](/notes/) collection. They appear alongside other notes in the main listing and can be filtered by category or tags.

## How to Add Reading Notes

Create reading notes directly in the `notes/` directory, just like any other note. They will automatically appear in the Notes listing.

## Reading Note Template

When adding reading notes, use this structure:

```markdown
---
title: "Paper/Book Title"
author: "Author Name"
year: YYYY
categories: ["reading"]  # Optional: use to group reading notes
tags: ["climate-science", "risk-modelling"]
date: YYYY-MM-DD
---

## Summary

Brief summary of key points and findings.

## Key Takeaways

- Point 1
- Point 2
- Point 3

## Connections

Links to related notes, models, or projects.

## Notes

Detailed notes, quotes, and reflections.
```

## Categories and Tags

Reading notes can be organized using categories and tags:

- **Categories**: Use `categories: ["reading"]` to group reading notes
- **Tags**: Add topic tags like `climate-science`, `energy-systems`, `risk-finance`, `methods`
- **Additional tags**: Consider `read`, `reading`, `to-read` for status tracking

## Suggested Starting Points

::: {.callout-tip}
## Getting Started with Climate Risk

- **IPCC AR6 Reports** - Essential background on climate science
- **IEA World Energy Outlook** - Annual energy sector analysis
- **Academic journals**: *Nature Climate Change*, *Energy Policy*, *Climatic Change*
:::

## Viewing Reading Notes

All reading notes appear in the [Notes & Knowledge Base](/notes/) listing. Use the category filter or search to find specific reading notes.
